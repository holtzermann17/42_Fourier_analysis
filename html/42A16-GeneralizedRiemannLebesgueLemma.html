Generalized Riemann-Lebesgue lemmaFernando Sanz Gamiz

Lemma 1.

Let h:R→C be a bounded measurable function. If h satisfies the averaging condition

limc→+∞⁡1c⁢∫0ch⁢(t)⁢𝑑t=0

then

limω→∞⁡∫abf⁢(t)⁢h⁢(ω⁢t)⁢𝑑t=0

with -∞<a<b<+∞ for any f∈L1⁢[a,b]

Proof.

Obviously we only need to prove the lemma when both h and f are real and 0=a<b<∞.

Let 𝟏[a,b] be the indicator function of the interval [a,b]. Then

limω→∞⁡∫0b𝟏[a,b]⁢h⁢(ω⁢t)⁢𝑑t=limω→∞⁡1ω⁢∫0ω⁢bh⁢(t)⁢𝑑t=0

by the hypothesis. Hence, the lemma is valid for indicators, therefore for step functions.

Now let C be a bound for h and choose ϵ >0. As step functions are dense in L1, we can find, for any f∈L1⁢[a,b], a step function g such that ∥f-g∥1<ϵ, therefore

limω→∞⁡|∫abf⁢(t)⁢h⁢(ω⁢t)⁢𝑑t| ⩽ limω→∞⁡∫ab|f⁢(t)-g⁢(t)|⁢|h⁢(ω⁢t)|⁢𝑑t+limω→∞⁡|∫abg⁢(t)⁢h⁢(ω⁢t)⁢𝑑t|
⩽ limω→∞⁡C⁢∥f-g∥1<C⁢ϵ

because limω→∞⁡|∫abg⁢(t)⁢h⁢(ω⁢t)⁢𝑑t|=0 by what we have proved for step functions. Since ϵ is arbitrary, we are done.

∎
