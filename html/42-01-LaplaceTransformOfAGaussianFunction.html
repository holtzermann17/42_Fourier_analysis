Laplace transform of a Gaussian function


We evaluate the Laplace transform 11cf. Gaussian function, wikipedia.org

ℒ⁢{e-t2}=∫0∞e-s⁢t⁢e-t2⁢𝑑t=F⁢(s). (1)

In fact,

ℒ⁢{e-t2}=∫0∞e-(t2+2⁢s2⁢t+s24-s24)⁢𝑑t=es24⁢∫0∞e-(t+s2)2⁢𝑑t.

By making the change of variable t+s2=u, we have (by the second equality in (1), the variable on operator’s argument is immaterial)

ℒ⁢{e-t2}=es24⁢∫s2∞e-u2⁢𝑑u.

That is,

ℒ⁢{e-t2}=F⁢(s)=π2⁢es24⁢erfc⁢(s2),

where erfc⁢(⋅) is the complementary error function. Its path of integration is subject to the restriction arg⁡(u)→θ, with |θ|≤π/4 as u→∞ along the path, with equality only if ℜ⁡(u2) remains bounded to the left.
